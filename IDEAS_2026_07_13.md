/ test-time compute / knowledge capacity / learning dynamics / systems / agentic

## Cross-lens convergences (independent lenses proposing the same thing)

1. **Selective token loss (Rho-1-style triage).** Training-data lens #1 AND
   optimization lens #2. Score every token with a reference model — either our
   ALREADY-STORED Qwen-7B KD logits (free) or SmolLM2-360M (1 day to score,
   cacheable) — and train only on learnable-not-yet-learned tokens; route
   teacher-KD onto informative tokens, drop noise. Rho-1: SOTA math at 3% of
   tokens. The single most direct attack on token poverty. Kill-test ~1 day.
2. **Decay-phase data upgrade (annealing).** Recipe lens' #1 ("the most
   replicated lever in every small-model report": SmolLM2 MATH 7→22 in decay;
   OpenCoder ablation-validated; OLMo-2 Dolmino; Qwen3 stage-2) + dynamics
   lens' knowledge-late placement + our own WSD structure making decay a
   cheap re-runnable appendix. We currently fill the decay with the SAME mix
   as the plateau — no strong recipe does this. Data downloadable
   (opc-annealing-corpus, Stack-Edu, FineMath4+). + OLMo-2 decay-soup (2-3
   decays, different order, soup) rides on our validated SWA result.
3. **Repair as the central untrained capability.** Test-time lens #1
   (repair-native decoding: sample→grade→feed error_text back→repair; the
   envelope-GROWER selection can't be) + agentic lens #1 (on-policy hindsight
   repair-traces as a ~15% pretrain STREAM, deployment loop then
   distribution-matched) + agentic #5 (repair-step DPO pairs free from the
   grader). All infra exists (gen_rejection_data --keep_all, error_text).
4. **SAM-on-WSD-decay.** Optimization #3 and dynamics #2 independently cite
   the same 2026 result (31% less SFT forgetting, 40% less quantization
   damage, OLMo-1B scale) — targets our chronic dirty-SFT regression + the
   quantized-edge-deploy story. 2x cost on only ~10-15% of steps. Stacks with
   SWA/soup.
5. **Recover the second GPU.** Systems #1: the DDP-latent incompatibility is
   a DistributedDataParallel-HOOK property, not a two-GPU property. Manual
   bucketed allreduce (no hooks, no graph assumptions; grad-accum amortizes
   comm to ~1-2%/step even unoverlapped) or DiLoCo local-SGD fallback.
   ~1.9x effective throughput = the largest single lever under token poverty.
   1-day decisive validation (bandwidth microbench + 500 real latent steps).
6. **Bounded-softmax hybrid, field-confirmed.** Architecture #1 (1-2
   sliding-window softmax layers, W=1024, placed by Gather-and-Aggregate head
   attribution — total state stays ~16MiB) + recipe lens part-3 warning:
   EVERY shipped efficient sub-1B line is hybrid (LFM2 ablated pure-linear
   OUT; Falcon-H1 "small attention fraction sufficient"). The field already
   answered our hybrid-tax fork: "hybrid, minimally." Harness exists.
7. **The latent executor as infrastructure, not headline.** Test-time #2
   (search-native decoding: state-checkpointed program tree search, 8.2MiB
   per branch vs GBs of KV — transformers' active pain point per ArborKV;
   partial-execution pruning + the neural interpreter as value function on
   non-executable prefixes = unpublished) + agentic #3 (simulate-or-shell-out
   router trained on free agreement labels latent-vs-real-execution =
   unpublished) + wildcard #4 (latent microcode: commit-op every ~4 hops
   writes result to state/WM, compose shallow units — attacks the 6-hop
   wall via state management) + wildcard #5 (co-execution DAgger; its FREE
   DIAGNOSTIC: measure hop-horizon under self-generated vs teacher-forced
   intermediates — the ~6-hop horizon may be exposure bias).
8. **The state as a first-class artifact.** Wildcard #1 (State Algebra:
   merge states from disjoint shards → parallel O(1) prefill, zero-training
   repo cartridges, crossover-as-search; State Soup validated the primitive
   on Mamba, delta-rule/repo/search versions open; <1-day probe on existing
   multibind harness; FAILURE IS EQUALLY LOAD-BEARING — order-entangled
   states would constrain the whole meta-TTT design) + knowledge #2 (state
   cartridges via self-study, the Cartridges recipe on an 8.2MiB flat state)
   + wildcard #6 (population-of-states + router).
9. **KD-native everything.** Recipe lens: Gemma-3 (KD for the whole line),
   LFM2 (KD for all 10T; decoupled top-K objective fixing truncated-KD
   support mismatch — drop-in for our bridge), Qwen3 (small models get
   strong-to-weak distillation NOT RL), MobileLLM-R1 ablation (SFT 74.0 vs
   RL 57.0). Converges with our own pass@k finding (RL = greedy sharpening
   only). Also: teacher COMPATIBILITY > teacher strength (a 1.5B coder

## Recipe-lens field facts worth pinning

- Nobody trains a competitive sub-1B on <2T tokens; we're at 5B → inheritance
  (linearization/KD) is the only coherent move — independently re-validated.
- RADLADS: softmax→linear conversion at 350-700M tokens retains ~90-100%;
  their ablations: hidden-state alignment step load-bearing; NEVER freeze
  MLPs; DCLM > FineWeb-Edu for Qwen teachers; reasoning teachers convert
  worse (measure inference-time-scaling retention after conversion — directly
  relevant to latent-thinking-on-linearized plans).
- Qwen2.5-Coder ablation: 70:20:10 code:text:math BEAT 85:10:5 and 100:0:0.
- FIM: universal (50% of code examples), ~free inside the decay mix, mostly
  benchmark-neutral but the editing capability is the agent north star.
- Deep-thin dominates sub-1B transformers — EXCEPT LFM2 (16L at 350M), the
  one line latency-optimized like us. Linearizing Qwen2.5-Coder-0.5B (24L)
  sidesteps the decision.
