# Future-direction evaluation (2026-06-28)

> Decision-ready strategy synthesis from a 10-agent adversarial workflow
> (4 evidence-mapping readers → 4 independent strategists with diverse lenses
> → an adversarial critic → a synthesizer). Ruthlessly honest, ranked,
> falsifiable. Cites our own results. The point is the one or two moves with a
> real chance of a defensible result on 2×RTX5090 — not "scale up + add tools."

## Bottom line

A "small super-coder that beats big models on coding benchmarks" on a **pure
DeltaNet** backbone at this budget is, on the accumulated evidence, **likely
structurally unreachable** — for two independent reasons no mechanism fixes:

1. **Token-poverty (unfixable on this rig).** SmolLM2-135M — *half* our params —
   models correct code better than our 287M (HumanEval-solution CE **0.798 vs
   0.969**). The gap is a ~400–800× token deficit (~5B vs 2–4T), not parameters,
   mechanisms, or data-mix. (`project_undertrained_not_undercapacity`.)
2. **A linear-attention tax that GROWS with donor strength.** Linearizing
   SmolLM2-360M cost +0.145 CE; the *stronger* Qwen2.5-Coder-0.5B donor cost
   **+0.276** and the student still landed at only 15/164. Inheritance buys a
   cost substrate, not a free quality jump, and we lose on quality to size-matched
   softmax models (SmolLM2-360M-Instruct **30/164** vs our **15**).

**So: stop chasing a HumanEval headline the tokens can't buy.** Spend the next
~2 weeks on cheap, mostly-training-free *measurements* that resolve the real
forks, then let those — not another benchmark chase — pick the headline.

## The two real forks

- **Is the DeltaNet linear-attention tax reducible?** If a few attention layers
  mixed back in (hybrid backbone) collapse the tax, inheritance becomes a quality
  win and the product goal is alive. If not, the pure-DeltaNet quality ceiling is
  structural → commit to a cost/efficiency or a science framing now.
- **Does latent thinking clear a fair control on a *real* depth-bound code
  benchmark?** If yes, it's a narrow capability; if no (the modal expectation),
  it's a clean publishable science result, not a product lever.

## Latent thinking — the verdict (the user's explicit question)

**Disposition: SALVAGE-NARROW.** Kill it as a general code/agentic/HumanEval
booster and **remove it from the deployed inference path**; keep it as (a) a
research asset and (b) gated on one decisive experiment.

**Proven, keep (high confidence):**
- Latent thinking substitutes trunk **depth** for **homogeneous iteration**:
  shallow L2 + R=K hits 1.00 through K=8, and off-diagonal R≠K collapses to chance
  → genuine per-step iteration, not memorization.
- The **structural Pareto floor** is solid: adapter-at-think-slot-only ⇒ no-think
  is byte-identical to base (max|Δlogits|=0 even at α=999); state-readonly β=0
  can't corrupt recall. Safe to *leave on* (≠ a reason it earns inference space).
- The **mechanistic diagnosis of why it fails on general tasks** — the
  heterogeneous-composition wall is a **per-step selection / credit-assignment
  limit**, not compute/depth/width. In-order programs fold for free; *random-access*
  selection collapses to chance for every *learned* final-answer-only selector,
  and only a fixed counter-gather (= an oracle handed the op identity) or per-hop
  process supervision recovers it. **This is the user's exact pain, as a result.**

**Refuted / over-claimed (do not quote):**
- "8→11/164 first thinking lift on code" — within ±3 greedy noise, never
  temp-confirmed.
- Latent-CoT distillation "+0.12 logp" — n=963 stratified probe found zero
  code-CE benefit in every difficulty bucket (the n=64 edge was self-caught noise).
- "Fix = a bolt-on op-selector" — a *learned* final-answer-only selector FAILS
  (0/8 at K6, α decays); only fixed-gather or process supervision works.

**The one experiment if salvaging:** process-supervised latent(R=n)
(teach-then-*freeze* selector, the existing `latent_trace_lineselect` recipe) on
~300–500 **CRUXEval output-prediction** items vs a **separately-trained**
`--no_think_frac 1.0` control (NOT the within-model `emit_threshold=0` path our own
fair-baseline mandate warns against), exact-match + bootstrap CI. **Modal
expectation: a clean null** → in which case it lands as the publishable
contribution, not a product lever.

## The strongest dissent worth preserving (novelty lens)

The most *defensible* deliverable here may be **neither the benchmark nor the
cost moat** (both lead to documented dead ends) but the **controlled scientific
characterization of when latent iteration substitutes for trunk depth in a
bounded-state recurrent LM**, with the heterogeneous-composition failure pinned
as a selection-learnability wall. That is verifiably novel beyond Coconut /
latent-CoT (which only show latent thinking *works*); the evidence is unusually
rigorous and code-reviewed (33-cell grid, fixed-gather==oracle, teach-then-wean
recovery tolerant to ≤30% label noise); and the tiny synthetic models are a
*strength* for a controlled study. **It is the only proactive positive deliverable
on the table that doesn't hinge on winning a benchmark the evidence says we lose.**

## Honest concessions (don't relitigate)

- Token-poverty is unfixable on this rig (half-size SmolLM2-135M beats us on
  code CE).
- The pure-DeltaNet tax grows with donor strength and dissipated the strongest
  donor's coding advantage — unless the hybrid/DeltaProduct ablation reduces it,
  inheritance buys cost substrate, not a quality jump.
- HumanEval-164 greedy at this scale is dominated by ±2–3 noise; the celebrated
  capability arc is largely a measurement artifact.
- The O(1)-decode cost moat is real-but-narrow ("efficient, not better"):
  production-stack incremental decode is **unwired**, recall *decays*, and a
  full-context transformer ties us at 4k.
- best-of-N+grader (~21) is a deployment win over our *own* greedy, not a
  competitive capability number.

## Bottom-line recommendation

Run the three cheap measurements (eval harness + CI; hybrid-tax ablation; honest
cost curve) before any new training campaign — the evidence already shows more
mechanism work and more RL won't move the goal. For **latent thinking** (the
user's question): kill it as a general code/agentic booster and pull it off the
inference path; keep the controlled depth-substitution characterization + the
per-step selection-learnability wall as the genuinely novel result, and gate any
remaining capability claim on the **CRUXEval vs fully-trained no-think** probe
(modal outcome: a clean null packaged as the science contribution). Deploy
best-of-N+grader (free) but always report it beside a matched-competitor arbiter,
and flip the per-head Newton-Schulz optimizer default-on. Let the **hybrid-tax
ablation** and the **CRUXEval gate** — not another HumanEval chase — decide
whether the headline becomes a *hybrid cost-competitive small coder* or a
*rigorous latent-depth science result*.
